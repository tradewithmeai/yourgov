Accessibility

YourGov should work for everyone who wants to see what their MP did. This page sets out where we are — and, more importantly, asks you to tell us where we fall short.

Status: we aim to meet WCAG 2.2 AA. Most of the service meets it today; some parts are still being improved (listed below). Last reviewed: June 2026.

What works today

  • You can find your MP and read their full voting record without using the map — by searching a postcode, constituency, or name, then keyboard-navigating the record.
  • A "Skip to main content" link, visible keyboard focus on every control, and proper page structure (headings and landmarks).
  • The map's colour modes (vote, party, gender, rebellion) pair every colour with a text label — colour is never the only signal.
  • We respect your device settings, including reduced motion. We do not use cookies to track you, and we never use a bolt-on "accessibility widget" — accessibility is built into the page itself.

What we're still improving

  • Deeper screen-reader support in the "Explain" panel and some dialogs.
  • An Easy Read version of how Parliament works, and British Sign Language video for key content.
  • Testing with more assistive technologies, and with disabled people directly.

Tell us what you need

If something on YourGov is hard to use — or you need it in a different format — please tell us. We genuinely don't know every barrier, and your message shapes what we build next. Email {{ feedback_email }}, or use the feedback page. We aim to reply within a few working days and can provide content in another format on request.

If we don't get it right

We want to hear from you first so we can fix it. If you're in Great Britain and remain unhappy with how we've responded, you can contact the Equality Advisory and Support Service (EASS), which advises on the Equality Act 2010.
